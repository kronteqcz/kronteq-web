# Competitor Research — Kronteq

## Summary

Primary successful research: **Gluetec Group** (gluetec-group.com). Sika and H.B. Fuller returned 403/404 for specific pages. Matrix a.s. turned out to be a different kind of holding (wood/automotive dealer, not a pure adhesives company).

---

## 1. Gluetec Group (gluetec-group.com) ✅

**Positioning:** "More than 25 years of expertise in industrial bonding and sealing products." Full-service partner: development + manufacturing + contract filling.

**Product portfolio:**
- Cyanoacrylates, epoxies, polyurethanes, MS polymers, anaerobics, silicones
- Sealants (PU, silicone)
- Aerosols (cleaning, lubricating, protective)
- Brands: WIKO® (mainstream), WELDYX® (industrial), PURYX® (polyurea)

**Industries:** Automotive, construction, woodworking, electronics, agriculture, rail, aerospace, marine

**Key USPs:**
- Custom formulation development
- Private label / contract filling
- User-friendly adhesive formulations
- Sustainability improvements in recent launches

**UX observations:**
- Navigation-heavy homepage with slider for new products
- Modular service sections
- Prominent brand showcase
- Downloads/datasheet repository
- Technical, professional tone — not overly corporate

**Takeaways for Kronteq:**
- Emphasise technical credentials prominently
- Provide product datasheets as downloads (trust signal)
- Show brands/certifications clearly
- Use "25+ years" or equivalent credibility markers

---

## 2. Sika (sika.com) — Partial

Global tier-1 chemical/adhesive supplier. Broad portfolio. Strong on environmental/sustainability messaging. Position: "More than adhesives — system solutions."

Key differentiator vs Kronteq: Sika is a megacorp. Kronteq can win on personalised service, speed, and Central European presence.

---

## 3. H.B. Fuller (hbfuller.com) — Partial

US-based industrial adhesive company, global. Heavy emphasis on "smart adhesive solutions", IoT-enabled dispensing systems. B2B buyer focus.

---

## 4. Matrix a.s. (matrix-as.cz)

Not a direct competitor. A Czech holding with automotive division that sells adhesives/surface treatments — but primarily a wood products company and Škoda dealer. Lower relevance.

---

## Key industry observations

1. **Technical credibility is table stakes** — datasheets, certifications, application notes all expected
2. **Industry-specific landing pages** — automotive, electronics, construction addressed separately
3. **"Partnership" language** dominates — not just selling products but providing solutions
4. **Fast delivery / stock availability** is a key differentiator for Czech/Central EU market
5. **Application support** (onsite engineers, training) is a strong USP for mid-tier specialists vs. distributors
